Frequently Asked Questions

  • I am 40years old and find it increasingly difficult to read prints in dim light. Why is this so?

    Answer: you most likely have presbyopia due to your loss of ability to adjust your eyes to near objects with age. Remedy is the use of spectacles for near work and reading.

  • Is my child not too young to wear glasses?

    Answer: a child is never too young to wear glasses, glasses help the child with poor vision to see clearly so their brains can develop properly during the critical period of brain development.

  • Is glaucoma inherited?

    Answer: yes, glaucoma can be inherited. Members of the same family have a higher chance of having glaucoma if either parent had glaucoma.

  • How can I avoid developing cataract?

    Answer: ensure adequate nutrition with fruits and vegetables, vitamins and protect your self from prolonged direct sunlight exposure. However cataract comes with age and if you live long enough may not be able to avoid it.

  • Why is glaucoma called the silent thief that steals your sight away?

    Answer: Glaucoma causes irreversible blindness without any warning signs. Only by accidental examination by a health personnel is it usually discovered. Therefore through routine and regular eye checkups can it be discovered on time.

  • Is conjunctivitis (appolo) infectious?

    Answer: yes it is. It can be prevented by avoiding direct contact with discharge from eyes of some one with it. Good personal hygiene and regular hands washing with soap and water help to reduce spread.

  • How can i book an appointment?

    Answer: Please contact us via email, phone number or come in person. All business information is on the contact us page.

  • Is it safe to apply urine to my eyes when I have red eyes?

    Answer: It is very dangerous to apply urine to the eyes because infection can be carried from the urine to the eyes and could result in the loss of the eye.

  • Is it safe to apply home remedies such as sugar water, salt solution, breast milk, break fluid, battery water etc to the eyes?

    Answer: It is very dangerous to apply any of these home remedies to the eyes. Some are poorly prepared and can contain bacteria which are harmful to the eyes. Breast milk and sugar water also provide nutrients for bacteria growth. Battery water is an acid and will damage the eyes.

  • What should I do if a chemical such as detergent, baking soda or acid water splash into my eyes?

    Answer: immediately irrigate the eyes with free flowing clean water for about 30min and thereafter see an eye doctor for further treatment.

  • Can using spectacles or eye drops cure cataract?

    Answer: No. The only effective treatment for cataract is by cataract operation.
